\section{Conclusions}\label{sec:conclusions}

We have demonstrated that autonomous AI agents violate all nine behavioral invariants upon which current banking fraud detection systems depend. This is not a contingent failure that can be patched with threshold adjustments or additional training data. It is a structural incompatibility: agents have no body to tire, no device to fingerprint, no geographic anchor, and near-zero marginal cost of identity creation. Every invariant---from velocity bounds to behavioral biometrics to KYC scarcity assumptions~\cite{bsa1970}---is an artifact of human physiology, psychology, or economics that ceases to hold when the transacting entity is software. Detection systems built on these invariants cannot distinguish legitimate agent commerce from agent-orchestrated fraud, not because they are poorly calibrated, but because the calibration target no longer exists.

The five-signal detection framework introduced in this paper addresses this structural gap by detecting what is \emph{present} in agent behavior but \emph{absent} in human behavior: deterministic network topology, machine-precision value flows, superhuman economic rationality, programmatic temporal patterns, and cross-platform behavioral inconsistencies. Validated on 81,904 real on-chain USDC transactions from 665 ERC-8004-registered agents~\cite{erc8004} sourced via Dune Analytics~\cite{dune2026}, the framework achieves 95.4\% recall on the raw evaluation set (81.1\% after label cleaning) with a transfer gap of only $-0.83$ percentage points from synthetic benchmarks. Seven of the eight attack chains defined in our threat taxonomy are detected at 100\% per-chain recall, including three scenarios rated \textsc{impossible} under conventional detection architectures.

The eighth chain---swarm intelligence attacks involving coordinated multi-agent populations that are individually indistinguishable from human users---reveals a fundamental architectural gap. Per-address scoring, no matter how sophisticated, cannot detect collective behavior that is designed to be individually invisible. Population-level detection operating at block-granularity time windows represents the next frontier, requiring new primitives for activation correlation, amount synchronization, and temporal clustering that no production system currently implements.

The window for proactive deployment is narrow. The full capability stack for agent-to-agent fraud---autonomous transaction execution, programmatic identity generation, and multi-agent coordination via platforms such as OpenClaw~\cite{openclaw2026}---has been operational since mid-2025. Historical fraud lags in DeFi (12--24 months) and NFT markets (12--18 months) suggest systematic A2A exploitation is likely within 12 months~\cite{virtuals2025}. The detection framework, open-source reference implementation, and institutional recommendations presented in this paper are designed for immediate deployment---before the structural vulnerabilities we have identified are exploited at scale.
